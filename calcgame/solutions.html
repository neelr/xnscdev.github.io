1. $\frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{y^2-3}{y(x-1)}$

$$\int\frac{y}{y^2-3}dy=\int\frac{1}{x-1}dx$$ Separation of variables
$$\frac{1}{2}\int\frac{2y}{y^2-3}dy=\int\frac{1}{x-1}dx$$
$$\frac{1}{2}\int\frac{1}{u}du=\int\frac{1}{v}dv$$ Substitute $u=y^2-3$, $du=2y\text{ }dy$, $v=x-1$, $dv=dx$
$$\frac{1}{2}\ln\left|y^2-3\right|=\ln\left|x-1\right|+c$$ Integrate and substitute back
$$e^{\ln\left|y^2-3\right|}=e^{2\ln\left|x-1\right|+2c}$$
$$y^2-3=Ce^{2\ln\left|x-1\right|}$$ Substitute $C=e^{2c}$
$$y^2-3=C(e^{\ln\left|x-1\right|})^2$$
$$y^2-3=C(x-1)^2$$
$$y=\pm\sqrt{C(x-1)^2+3}$$ General solution
$$2=\pm\sqrt{C(2-1)^2+3}$$ Plug $(2,2)$ into general solution to solve for particular solution
$$2=\sqrt{C+3}$$ Remove $\pm$ because 2 is positive
$$4=C+3$$
$$C=1$$ Substitute into general solution equation
$$y=\sqrt{(x-1)^2+3}$$ Particular solution through $(2,2)$

2. $\frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{\cos x}{e^y}$

$$\int e^y dy=\int\cos x\text{ }dx$$ Separation of variables
$$e^y=\sin x+C$$ Integrate
$$\ln e^y=\ln(\sin x+C)$$
$$y=\ln(\sin x+C)$$ General solution
$$1=\ln(\sin\pi+C)$$ Plug $(\pi,1)$ into general solution to solve for particular solution
$$e=\sin\pi+C$$
$$C=e$$ Substitute into general solution equation
$$y=\ln(\sin x+e)$$ Particular solution through $(\pi,1)$

3. $\frac{dy}{dx}=x(5-y)$

$$\int\frac{1}{5-y}dy=\int x\text{ }dx$$ Separation of variables
$$-\int-\frac{1}{5-y}dy=\int x\text{ }dx$$
$$-\int\frac{1}{u}du=\int x\text{ }dx$$ Substitute $u=5-y$, $du=-dy$
$$-\ln\left|5-y\right|=\frac{1}{2}x^2+c$$ Integrate and substitute back
$$e^{-\ln\left|5-y\right|}=e^{\frac{1}{2}x^2+c}$$
$$5-y=e^{-\frac{1}{2}x^2-c}$$
$$y=5-Ce^{-\frac{1}{2}x^2}$$ Substitute $C=e^{-c}$, General solution
$$6=5-Ce^{-\frac{1}{2}(\sqrt{2})^2}$$ Plug in $(\sqrt 2,6)$ into general solution to solve for particular solution
$$6=5-Ce^{-1}$$
$$Ce^{-1}=-1$$
$$C=-e$$
$$y=5-(-e)(e^{-\frac{1}{2}x^2})$$ Substitute into general solution equation
$$y=5+e^{-\frac{1}{2}x^2+1}$$ Particular solution through $(\sqrt 2,6)$

4. $\frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{x^2+3x}{1+2y}$

$$\int(1+2y)dy=\int(x^2+3x)dx$$ Separation of variables
$$y+y^2=\frac{1}{3}x^3+\frac{3}{2}x^2+c$$ Integrate
$$y^2+y+\frac{1}{4}=\frac{1}{3}x^3+\frac{3}{2}x^2+C$$ Substitute $C=c+\frac{1}{4}$
$$\Big(y+\frac{1}{2}\Big)^2=\frac{1}{3}x^3+\frac{3}{2}x^2+C$$ Complete the square on $y$
$$y+\frac{1}{2}=\pm\sqrt{\frac{1}{3}x^3+\frac{3}{2}x^2+C}$$
$$y=\pm\sqrt{\frac{1}{3}x^3+\frac{3}{2}x^2+C}-\frac{1}{2}$$ General solution
$$\frac{1}{2}=\pm\sqrt{\frac{1}{3}(1)^3+\frac{3}{2}(1)^2+C}- \frac{1}{2}$$ Plug in $(1,\frac{1}{2})$ into general solution to solve for particular solution
$$1=\frac{1}{3}+\frac{3}{2}+C$$
$$C=1-\frac{11}{6}$$
$$C=-\frac{5}{6}$$ Substitute into general solution equation
$$y=\sqrt{\frac{1}{3}x^3+\frac{3}{2}x^2-\frac{5}{6}}-\frac{1}{2} $$ Particular solution through $(1,\frac{1}{2})$

5. $\frac{dy}{dx}=y\sin^2x$

$$\int\frac{1}{y}dy=\int\sin^2x\text{ }dx$$ Separation of variables
$$\int\frac{1}{y}dy=\int\frac{1-\cos2x}{2}dx$$ Double angle formula
$$\int\frac{1}{y}dy=\frac{1}{2}\int(1-\cos2x)dx$$
$$\int\frac{1}{y}dy=\frac{1}{2}\int dx-\frac{1}{4}\int2\cos2x \text{ }dx$$
$$\int\frac{1}{y}dy=\frac{1}{2}\int dx-\frac{1}{4}\int\cos u \text{ }du$$ Substitute $u=2x$, $du=2dx$
$$\ln\left|y\right|=\frac{1}{2}x-\frac{1}{4}\sin2x+c$$ Integrate and substitute back
$$e^{\ln\left|y\right|}=Ce^{\frac{1}{2}x-\frac{1}{4}\sin2x}$$ Substitute $C=e^c$
$$y=Ce^{\frac{1}{2}x-\frac{1}{4}\sin2x}$$ General solution
$$-e=Ce^{\frac{1}{2}(0)-\frac{1}{4}\sin2(0)}$$ Plug in $(0,-e)$ into general solution to solve for particular solution
$$-e=Ce^0$$
$$C=-e$$
$$y=(-e)(e^{\frac{1}{2}x-\frac{1}{4}\sin2x})$$ Substitute into general solution equation
$$y=-e^{\frac{1}{2}x-\frac{1}{4}\sin2x+1}$$ Particular solution through $(0,-e)$

6. $\frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{x^3+2}{\sin y}$

$$\int\sin y\text{ }dy=\int(x^3+2)dx$$ Separation of variables
$$-\cos y=\frac{1}{4}x^4+2x+C$$ Integrate
$$\cos y=-\frac{1}{4}x^4-2x-C$$
$$y=\cos^{-1}\Big(-\frac{1}{4}x^4-2x-C\Big)$$ General solution
$$\frac{\pi}{2}=\cos^{-1}\Big(-\frac{1}{4}(0)^4-2(0)-C\Big)$$ Plug in $(0,\frac{\pi}{2})$ into general solution to solve for particular solution
$$\cos\frac{\pi}{2}=-C$$
$$C=0$$ Substitute into general solution equation
$$y=\cos^{-1}\Big(-\frac{1}{4}x^4-2x\Big)$$ Particular solution through $(0,\frac{\pi}{2})$

7. A 120°F object in a 70°F room cools to 100°F in 5 minutes.

$$\frac{dy}{dx}=k(y-70)$$ Apply Newton's law of cooling
$$\int\frac{1}{y-70}dy=\int k\text{ }dx$$ Separation of variables
$$\int\frac{1}{u}du=\int k\text{ }dx$$ Substitute $u=y-70$, $du=dy$
$$\ln\left|y-70\right|=kx+c$$ Integrate and substitute back
$$e^{\ln\left|y-70\right|}=e^{kx+c}$$
$$y-70=Ce^{kx}$$ Substitute $C=e^c$
$$y=Ce^{kx}+70$$ General solution before solving for $k$
$$120=Ce^0+70$$ Solve for $C$ using $x=0$, $y=120$ (object is initially 120°F)
$$C=50$$
$$100=50e^{5k}+70$$ Substitute $C$ to solve for $k$ (object is 100°F after 5 minutes)
$$\frac{3}{5}=e^{5k}$$
$$\ln\frac{3}{5}=5k$$
$$k=\frac{\ln\frac{3}{5}}{5}$$ Substitute $C$ and $k$ for general and particular solutions
$$y=Ce^{\frac{x\ln\frac{3}{5}}{5}}+70$$ General solution
$$y=50e^{\frac{x\ln\frac{3}{5}}{5}}+70$$ Particular solution through $(5,100)$
